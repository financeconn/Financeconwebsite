Price Elasticity of Demand (PED)

Microeconomics

Measures how much the quantity demanded of a good responds to a change in its price.

PED = (% Change in Quantity Demanded) / (% Change in Price)

Interpretation:

  • ‚Ä¢ |PED| > 1: Elastic demand (sensitive to price changes)
  • ‚Ä¢ |PED| = 1: Unit elastic demand
  • ‚Ä¢ |PED| < 1: Inelastic demand (less sensitive to price changes)

PED = -15% / 10% = -1.5

The demand is elastic (|PED| > 1), meaning consumers are relatively sensitive to price changes.

Marginal Cost (MC)

Microeconomics

The additional cost incurred from producing one more unit of a good or service.

MC = ŒîTC / ŒîQ

Where:

  • ‚Ä¢ ŒîTC = Change in total cost
  • ‚Ä¢ ŒîQ = Change in quantity produced

üìù Example:

If total cost increases from $1,000 to $1,100 when production increases from 100 to 110 units:

MC = ($1,100 - $1,000) / (110 - 100) = $100 / 10 = $10

The marginal cost of producing each additional unit is $10.

Gross Domestic Product (GDP)

Macroeconomics

The total monetary value of all finished goods and services produced within a country's borders in a specific time period.

GDP = C + I + G + (X - M)

Where:

  • ‚Ä¢ C = Consumption (household spending)
  • ‚Ä¢ I = Investment (business spending)
  • ‚Ä¢ G = Government spending
  • ‚Ä¢ X = Exports
  • ‚Ä¢ M = Imports
  • ‚Ä¢ (X - M) = Net exports

üìù Example:

For a country with:

Exchange Rate Formula

International

The rate at which one currency can be exchanged for another.

Exchange Rate = Currency A / Currency B

üìù Example:
